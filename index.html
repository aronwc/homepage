Dr. Culotta leads the Text Analysis in the Public Interest Lab, which investigates socially-beneficial applications of natural language processing, machine learning, and text mining algorithms. See the Projects page for more information. The lab focuses on three core areas:

  • Social Media Analysis: Our work has found surprising correlations between Twitter chatter and values such as the influenza rate, alcohol sales volume, and the level of concern towards an impending hurricane.
  • Information extraction and Data mining: We have investigated several statistical algorithms to extract facts and patterns from unstructured text, focusing on the fundamental problems of coreference resolution, relation extraction, and canonicalization.
  • Machine learning: The difficulty of the above applications has driven research in novel graphical modeling methods that are built to scale to problems with a large number of complex dependencies. We have also invented new active learning algorithms specifically tailored for these tasks.
Press: Our work in social media analysis has been discussed in several press outlets, including the Wall Street Journal, The Atlantic, CNET, and the Communcations of the ACM.
