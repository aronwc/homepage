I investigate computational methods to learn about human behavior from online social networks.

Examples include tracking diseases, measuring effectiveness of public health campaigns, informing crisis response, preventing online harassment, detecting deceptive marketing, and identifying unsafe products.

The methods rely on machine learning, natural language processing, and social network analysis. Areas of technical contribution include domain adaptation, learning from label proportions, and causal inference. See our lab page for more.

I am always open to collaboration, particularly with social scientists.
